# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Construction project managers at the general contractor — running multiple active jobs from a laptop at a desk, occasionally checking in from a jobsite. Their day is a constant switch between budget, schedule, RFIs, submittals, change orders, and vendor communications. They came from Procore (or a competitor) and feel its weight every day: too many clicks, too much chrome, too little signal. They are senior operators who trust their own judgment; the tool should respect that.

## Product Purpose

Alleato PM is the construction project management platform that replaces Procore for owner's-rep and GC workflows — budgets, prime contracts, commitments, change orders, RFIs, submittals, directory, scheduling, and AI-assisted comms intelligence. It exists because the incumbent is slow, cluttered, and forces operators to hunt for what matters. Success is when a PM opens a project and the next decision is obvious in under five seconds — what's overdue, what's at risk, what's waiting on them — without scrolling, without dashboards-of-dashboards, without modals.

## Brand Personality

Sharp. Fast. Modern. The voice is operator-to-operator — no marketing register inside the app, ever. Confident enough to omit explanation when the UI is self-evident. Quiet enough that the data does the talking. The product should feel engineered by people who actually run construction jobs, not designed by an agency.

## Anti-references

- **Procore itself.** Cluttered enterprise SaaS — gray-on-gray toolbars, 90s-era forms, dense chrome, modal-heavy flows, dated typography. Every choice should be a quiet rejection of how Procore feels.
- **Generic admin templates** (Material Dashboard, TailAdmin, Bootstrap admin). Bland card grids, hero-metric templates, identical KPI boxes, "Welcome back!" greetings. The shape of slop.
- **Consumer-friendly / playful.** No bright illustrations, no gamification, no marketing-y empty states with cartoon characters. This is a serious tool for serious work — the personality comes from precision, not whimsy.
- **Heavy chrome / skeuomorphic.** No drop shadows beyond `shadow-xs`/`shadow-sm`, no decorative gradients, no glassmorphism, no bevels, no trend-chasing visual flourishes.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Every pixel earns its place by helping the user see what matters. If it doesn't carry information or hierarchy, it shouldn't be on screen. Whitespace and typography do the work that borders and cards try to do badly.
2. **Information density without noise.** Pack more truth into less space than Procore ever could — through scale contrast, alignment, and tonal restraint. Density and calm are not opposites when hierarchy is right.
3. **The next decision should be obvious.** A PM glancing at any page should know within five seconds what action to take. Surface what's at risk, overdue, or waiting; demote everything else.
4. **Trust the operator.** Don't over-explain, don't hand-hold, don't wrap obvious actions in confirmation modals. Senior PMs know their job — the tool should keep up, not slow them down.
5. **Speed is a feature.** Keyboard-fluent, snappy interactions, no unnecessary loading states. The product should feel faster than the user expects every single time.

## Positive References

Linear (information density, calm gray-on-white, side-nav structure), Superhuman (speed-of-thought interactions, dense lists with subtle hierarchy, premium restraint), Notion/Height (flexible inline editing, document-meets-database feel, calm whitespace).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA is the floor — no exceptions. Desktop-first layouts that remain fully usable on mobile (grids collapse, tables horizontal-scroll, layouts stack). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for every animation. Color is never the sole carrier of meaning — pair with icon, label, or position. Keyboard navigation is a first-class citizen, not an afterthought.
