# Episode Focus: Marketing Agent And Accounting Automation

## What The AI Hosts Should Focus On

This episode should be a follow-up to the broad Alleato AI overview. The first overview already covered the main AI assistant vision, project intelligence, and construction workflow roadmap. This episode should fill in two important areas that were under-covered:

1. The content marketing / CMO agent plan.
2. The accounting, Acumatica, PSR, WIP, and real-time financial reporting system.

## Suggested Episode Flow

### 1. Open With The Bigger Company Operating System Idea

Frame Alleato AI as more than a project-management assistant. It is becoming a company operating system with specialized agents for project execution, finance/accounting, risk, business development, and marketing.

Key line:

> The same AI foundation that helps catch project risk can also help generate marketing momentum and keep accounting numbers current without waiting for manual monthly reporting.

Explain that the marketing agent is mostly a roadmap/implementation plan, not a completed feature yet.

The CMO agent is intended to become a strategic growth operator, not just a content bot. It should:

- Monitor market and competitor positioning.
- Identify content opportunities.

- Build content calendars.

Business value:

- More consistent content output.
- Stronger authority in design-build construction.
- Better use of internal knowledge and project stories.
- Less dependency on ad hoc marketing effort.
- A repeatable growth engine that compounds over time.

### 4. Accounting And Acumatica: What Is Already Much Further Along

Make clear that accounting is not just a future idea. A lot is already built.

Existing or documented capabilities include:

- AI financial advisor / CFO advisor.
- Live Acumatica ERP access for AP aging, AR aging, cash position, vendor spend, bills, invoices, purchase orders, and project budgets.
- Financial tools for commitments, direct costs, change orders, budget line items, cost trends, and margin analysis.
- Direct costs synced from Acumatica AP bills.
- Read-only policy for payment/direct-cost records that should stay controlled by Acumatica.
- Project Status Report (PSR) feature plan and implementation path to replace monthly manual accounting report assembly.
- WIP report implementation using Acumatica project budgets, AR invoices, and project metadata.
- Accounting dashboard surfaces for AR/AP aging, cash position, recent payments/checks, and guardrail alerts.

Business value:

- Brandon and accounting can see fresher financial truth.
- Less manual report assembly.
- Fewer month-end surprises.
- Faster visibility into overbilling, underbilling, cost-to-complete, margin, AP/AR, and cash.
- Acumatica remains the accounting source of truth while Alleato makes the data easier to understand and act on.

### 5. Explain PSR In Plain English

The Project Status Report is the report accounting currently assembles manually every month. The goal is to make it live and always-current.

It pulls from project data already in Alleato:

- Project information.
- Budget detail.
- Submittals.
- RFIs.
- Change requests.
- Change orders.
- Schedule.
- Monthly billing.
- PM/accounting comments.

Business value:

- Instead of waiting for a manually assembled report, the team can open a project and see the current status instantly.
- It supports owner conversations, internal project reviews, and accounting visibility.
- PDF export keeps the familiar reporting format while the data becomes live.

### 6. Explain WIP In Plain English

The WIP report helps answer: Are we overbilled, underbilled, profitable, and on track by project?

It uses:

- Acumatica project budget lines.
- Acumatica AR invoices.
- Acumatica project metadata.

It calculates:

- Contract value.
- Revised cost budget.
- Costs to date.
- Committed costs.
- Open commitments.
- Cost to complete.
- Estimated final cost / EAC.
- Percent complete.
- Earned revenue.
- Billed to date.
- Over/under billing.
- Forecast gross profit.
- Forecast gross margin.

Key technical detail for Brandon:

The current WIP math is percentage-of-completion based:

```text
percent complete = costs to date / estimated final cost
earned revenue = contract value x percent complete
over/under billing = billed to date - earned revenue
```

Business value:

- Leadership can see which jobs are underbilled or overbilled.
- Accounting can review revenue recognition and billing position faster.
- PMs can see whether cost-to-complete or EAC needs attention.

### 7. Explain The Acumatica Relationship

Acumatica remains the accounting system of record. Alleato does not need to replace Acumatica. The value is that Alleato mirrors, analyzes, and explains the accounting data in the context of project operations.

Important distinction:

- Acumatica owns official accounting records.
- Alleato connects those numbers to project context, commitments, change orders, direct costs, invoices, PSRs, WIP, and AI explanations.

That makes the numbers easier to use day-to-day without weakening accounting control.

### 8. Recommended Next Steps

Recommended next steps for Brandon to hear:

1. Treat marketing/CMO as the next AI expansion lane after the core assistant and financial foundation.
2. Pilot the CMO agent around one concrete workflow: turn project wins, owner updates, or leadership thoughts into a weekly content calendar and content drafts.
3. Continue maturing accounting automation around PSR, WIP, direct-cost sync, and Acumatica freshness.
4. Prioritize owner-facing financial visibility: live PSR, WIP, cash position, AR/AP aging, and change-order exposure.
5. Keep the approval model clear: AI explains, drafts, flags, and recommends; people approve official financial or external actions.
