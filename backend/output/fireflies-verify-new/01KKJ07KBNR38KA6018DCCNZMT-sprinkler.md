## **Fire Suppression System Cost Drivers**

The team clarified why Varsity Brands’ fire suppression quote is higher due to height inefficiencies and system design constraints.

- **Height inefficiency inflates cost by a third** (03:54)  
    - The system is **34 feet tall**, just over the threshold for two levels, forcing three levels of sprinklers.  
    - ESFR code requires sprinkler lines every **10 feet**, with protection only covering **4 feet** of storage above, causing an extra level.  
    - This inefficiency skews the cost per tote significantly upward for Varsity Brands.  
    - Brandon explained that if the system were shorter by about **4 feet**, two levels would suffice, reducing costs by roughly a third.

- **Two separate systems increase installation time and cost** (05:22)  
    - Kebba confirmed the system includes **two feeders**, requiring separate installations and increasing duration.  
    - Even though the square footage is not large, splitting the system extends installation time.  
    - This design differs from other projects that had single feeder lines, raising complexity.  
    - The dual-system design is a key factor behind the higher price.

- **Code and design constraints limit efficiency options** (10:05)  
    - The team reviewed sprinkler placement options: every **10 feet** or every **15 feet** with upsized pipes.  
    - Exotech’s system design limits pipe size upsizing, forcing more frequent sprinkler levels.  
    - Brandon stressed the need for early design collaboration to improve efficiency but noted Exotech typically resists design changes.  
    - Stephen agreed that without early involvement, the current cost structure is unavoidable.

- **Water pressure and system layout impact feeder lines** (24:06)  
    - Water pressure availability and system layout affect whether one or two feeder lines are needed.  
    - Varsity Brands requires two feeders due to an access gap, unlike McLean, which uses one.  
    - The pressure and manifold versus loop system design drive complexity and cost.  
    - This nuance explains why Varsity Brands has twice as many drops despite similar tote and level counts.

## **Comparative Project Pricing Insights**

- **Superior Beverage’s system evolved from two to three levels** (15:50)

    - Initial quotes underestimated the system height, leading to inaccurate pricing.  
    - Brandon recalled that the original price was based on verbal height info, not detailed CAD files.  
    - This example showed how early assumptions on height impact pricing accuracy.

- **Need to verify design files and ceiling heights for accuracy** (18:36)

    - Brandon agreed to review their files for possible discrepancies.  
    - Clarification of ceiling height differences is critical as even a few feet alter sprinkler requirements.  
    - This verification is key to preventing pricing mismatches in future quotes.

- **Front-end estimates need updating with better fire suppression knowledge** (28:52)  
    - Alex acknowledged the front-end team underestimated costs by about **30-50%** due to lack of detailed system understanding.  
    - Past rule-of-thumb pricing does not hold for these exotic systems anymore.  
    - The team agreed to educate application engineers on key drivers like sprinkler levels and spacing intervals.  
    - This will help produce more accurate upfront pricing and reduce surprises later.
