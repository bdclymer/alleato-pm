## **AI Integration and Business Pivot**

The team agreed on shifting business focus from tech-heavy clients to non-tech sectors due to competitive AI developments and client self-sufficiency.

- **Amit Patel identified a revenue challenge** as tech clients reduce outsourcing, building in-house AI solutions that are less enterprise-grade but cheaper, causing lost deals (49:40).
    - This shift forced Amit to redefine the ideal customer profile toward non-tech sectors like healthcare and banking.
    - He is pursuing partnerships to offer AI implementation services to new industries lacking deep AI expertise.
    - This pivot aims to capture markets where clients cannot easily replicate AI capabilities internally.
    - Brandon Tigges supported this approach as a necessary response to the evolving AI market dynamics.

- **Brandon Tigges and Amit discussed the unsustainable AI cost model** of major AI firms, predicting an imminent bubble burst due to massive financial losses and rising token costs (52:15).
    - They expect token prices to rise by at least **10x**, forcing organizations to ration AI usage carefully.
    - Businesses will need discipline on AI spending, balancing human labor and AI costs for efficiency.
    - Amit compared this to cloud adoption, where initial cost underestimation led to hybrid infrastructure solutions.
    - The conversation highlighted the need for enterprise-grade AI management, security, and compliance to avoid costly failures.

- **Amit emphasized the underestimated management complexity** required to deploy multi-agent AI systems at scale (55:12).
    - Even with aggressive staff cuts aided by AI, competitors will adopt similar strategies, leading to a need to rebuild teams to maintain advantage.
    - Amit projects a cyclical pattern where initial AI cuts are followed by re-expansion due to operational demands.
    - Brandon noted security challenges, calling AI infrastructure a hacker’s target requiring strict controls.
    - Both foresee a future where AI agents augment rather than replace human roles, especially in strategic positions.

    - Amit and Brandon foresee a tech bubble disruption more severe than the 2008 financial crisis, driven by AI economics.
