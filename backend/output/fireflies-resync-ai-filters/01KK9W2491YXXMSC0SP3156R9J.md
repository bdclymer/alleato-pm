    - Payment terms aim to balance cash flow concerns while securing exclusivity and production commitments
- **Minimum order quantity clarified as firm, not flexible** to reflect realistic production scale and growth expectations (04:15)

    - The payment and exclusivity terms were designed based on feedback to ease cash flow pressure while preserving commitment

The supply chain strategy integrates transit buffers, certification timelines, and production scheduling to meet market demands efficiently.

- **Supply chain timeline includes metal procurement and production lead times** totaling about eight months from order to shipment (24:42)

    - Continuous production and regular certification audits expected to smooth ongoing manufacturing
- **Certification plans cover CEC and Florida product approvals** to open new markets and extend selling seasons (02:12)
    - Certification process expected to be integrated into continuous production with re-audits rather than one-time approvals
    - This approach is designed to reduce future certification delays and enable sales in states with winter installations
- **Production scheduling prioritizes consecutive monthly runs** to minimize changeover costs and maintain stable pricing (19:30)

    - Payment schedule aligned with production months to optimize cash flow and manufacturing startup costs
    - Flexibility exists to adjust production months based on evolving pipeline needs, pending order timing confirmation
